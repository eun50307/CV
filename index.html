About Me

I am a Ph.D. student in environmental psychology and acoustics at ENTPE (École Nationale des Travaux Publics de l’État) in Lyon, France. My research focuses on the relationship between noise sensitivity, noise annoyance, and physiological responses such as facial electromyography (fEMG), electrodermal activity (EDA), and pupillometry. I am particularly interested in the psychometric validation of noise sensitivity instruments and in understanding how individual differences shape perceptual and physiological reactions to environmental noise.
